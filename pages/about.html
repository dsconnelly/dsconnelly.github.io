As of June 2026, I will be a postdoctoral researcher at the University of California, Berkeley working with William Boos to improve prediction of the East African and South Asian monsoon systems. More broadly, I am interested in integrating physics-based and data-driven approaches to atmospheric dynamics across scales.

Advised by Edwin P. Gerber, I completed my PhD at the Center for Atmosphere Ocean Science within the Courant Institute at New York University. In my dissertation, I coupled machine learning with high-fidelity physical modeling to build better parameterizations of inertia-gravity waves in atmospheric models.

I received a bachelor's in mathematics from Cornell University. There, and during two internships at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, I conducted research with Natalie Mahowald and David R. Thompson on remote sensing and modeling of desert dust aerosols. In the spring of 2026, I returned to Ithaca as a Visiting Lecturer of atmospheric dynamics in the Department of Earth and Atmospheric Sciences.

Beyond academic life, I am a musician, a baker, and an avid soccer fan. I enjoy hiking and cycling when the weather is fair. I'm also fascinated by linguistics, especially phonetics and historical reconstruction.
